Proposed NeurIPS 2026 Workshop

Foundation Models and Generative AI for Aging Science

ML for aging biology: virtual cells, perturbations, and intervention modeling.

An informal venue for discussion, community building, and work in progress at the intersection of machine learning and aging science.

Status: Workshop proposal in preparation. NeurIPS decisions expected July 11, 2026.

About

Why now?

Machine learning is entering a new phase in biology. Large-scale single-cell atlases, perturbation datasets, biological foundation models, and virtual-cell systems are making it possible to model cellular state, simulate interventions, and ask whether AI systems can help explain and modify biological processes rather than only classify them.

Aging science is a natural but underdeveloped frontier for this shift. Aging is multi-scale, dynamic, heterogeneous, and intervention-driven: it unfolds across molecules, cells, tissues, organs, organisms, and time. It is also deeply connected to major ML challenges, including representation learning, causal inference, uncertainty quantification, multimodal learning, generative modeling, experimental design, and evaluation under distribution shift.

This workshop aims to build a focused community around AI for aging biology. We will bring together machine learning researchers, computational biologists, aging biologists, and translational scientists to discuss how AI can help model aging, predict responses to interventions, evaluate biological age and healthspan biomarkers, and design more reliable biological discovery systems.

While recent AI-biology workshops have focused on virtual cells and drug discovery pipelines, this workshop centers aging biology and aging-relevant intervention modeling as a distinct scientific and machine learning problem. Our goal is not a talks-only program, but a forum for discussion, contributed work, open problems, benchmarks, and community building.

Topics

Areas of interest

We welcome work on machine learning methods, datasets, benchmarks, and position papers related to:

  • Foundation models for aging biology, single-cell systems, tissues, and multimodal biological data
  • Virtual cells and biological world models for aging, disease progression, rejuvenation, and intervention response
  • Perturbation response prediction across genetic, chemical, environmental, and reprogramming interventions
  • Generative models for intervention design, including small molecules, genetic perturbations, cell-state transitions, and combination therapies
  • Aging clocks, biological age predictors, healthspan biomarkers, and uncertainty-aware biomarker modeling
  • Causal inference, counterfactual prediction, and experimental design for aging-relevant biology
  • Multimodal learning across transcriptomics, epigenomics, proteomics, imaging, spatial biology, clinical phenotypes, and longitudinal data
  • Modeling cellular senescence, inflammation, loss of proteostasis, mitochondrial dysfunction, epigenetic change, and other hallmarks of aging
  • Evaluation frameworks for biological foundation models, including robustness, calibration, biological validity, perturbation generalization, and mechanistic alignment
  • Agentic AI systems and automated scientific discovery for aging biology
  • Reproducibility, open datasets, benchmarks, responsible deployment, and translational considerations in AI for aging science

We welcome research, position, dataset/benchmark, and systems contributions on AI for aging and intervention modeling. We will not accept work already published at major ML venues or presented at the NeurIPS 2026 main conference.

Format (planned): posters for accepted papers; a subset invited for spotlight talks.
