Why this workshop?

A new ML community for aging biology and intervention modeling

Recent advances in foundation models, generative modeling, multimodal learning, causal inference, and agentic scientific systems are transforming biology. Longevity science presents a uniquely challenging frontier: aging is a multi-scale, dynamic, heterogeneous biological process, and interventions require reasoning under uncertainty, limited observability, and expensive experimental validation.

This workshop aims to establish a rigorous interdisciplinary venue for machine learning researchers, computational biologists, systems biologists, and biomedical scientists working on AI methods that can model aging, predict cellular responses, and accelerate discovery of interventions for age-related decline and disease.

Core thesis

Longevity science is a frontier ML problem.

Progress will require new methods for multimodal representation learning, virtual-cell simulation, perturbation prediction, causal intervention discovery, uncertainty-aware modeling, and biologically grounded evaluation.

Topics of Interest

We welcome work across methods, systems, datasets, and scientific applications.

Biological foundation models

Models for transcriptomics, imaging, proteins, epigenomics, spatial biology, and multimodal cellular states.

Virtual cells and world models

Predictive simulators for cellular dynamics, perturbations, differentiation, senescence, and rejuvenation.

Perturbation response prediction

ML methods for CRISPR, chemical, transcription-factor, and combinatorial interventions.

Generative intervention design

Diffusion, flow matching, latent dynamical systems, and optimization for candidate interventions.

Causal ML for biology

Causal representation learning, counterfactual prediction, intervention discovery, and experimental design.

Aging clocks and biomarkers

Epigenetic, transcriptomic, proteomic, imaging, and functional biomarkers for aging and rejuvenation.

Agentic AI for science

AI systems for literature synthesis, hypothesis generation, wet-lab planning, and closed-loop discovery.

Evaluation and reproducibility

Benchmarks, negative results, uncertainty, robustness, dataset shift, and biologically meaningful metrics.

Call for Papers

Submission tracks

We invite submissions that advance AI methods for aging biology, longevity science, and intervention modeling. Accepted papers will be presented as posters; selected papers will be invited for spotlight talks.

  • Research papers: novel methods, models, theory, or empirical studies.
  • Position papers: open problems, benchmarks, evaluation gaps, and community roadmaps.
  • Dataset and benchmark papers: resources for aging, perturbation, multimodal biology, or virtual-cell evaluation.
  • Systems papers: scalable infrastructure, agentic scientific workflows, reproducibility, and deployment lessons.

Submission details: TBD after workshop acceptance. We expect a non-archival format, double-blind review, and OpenReview submission.
