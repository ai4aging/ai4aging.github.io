Proposed NeurIPS 2026 Workshop

Foundation Models and Generative AI for Aging Science

ML for aging biology: virtual cells, perturbations, and intervention modeling.

An informal venue for discussion, community building, and work in progress at the intersection of machine learning and aging science.

Status: Workshop proposal in preparation. NeurIPS decisions expected July 11, 2026.

About

Recent advances in foundation models, generative AI, and large-scale biological datasets are transforming how we model cells, predict interventions, and study disease. At the same time, aging science has emerged as a major scientific frontier, driven by new aging biomarkers, cellular reprogramming, large perturbation datasets, and growing interest in understanding the mechanisms of aging.

Despite this momentum, there is currently no dedicated venue at NeurIPS focused on the unique machine learning challenges of aging biology and interventions for aging.

Aging is not a single disease. It is a dynamic, multi-scale process spanning molecules, cells, tissues, and organisms over time. Addressing it requires advances in representation learning, causal reasoning, generative modeling, uncertainty quantification, and experimental design.

This workshop brings together researchers from machine learning, computational biology, and aging research to discuss how AI can help model aging, predict intervention outcomes, discover new biological mechanisms, and accelerate progress toward healthier aging.

Topics

Areas of interest

  • Foundation models for biology and aging
  • Virtual cells and biological world models
  • Perturbation and intervention modeling
  • Aging clocks and biological age prediction
  • Multimodal and longitudinal biological data
  • Causal inference and experimental design
  • AI-driven discovery for aging and rejuvenation
  • Benchmarks, evaluation, and reproducibility

Call for Papers

Contributions (after acceptance)

Submission details will be posted here if the workshop is accepted. We plan to use OpenReview, with non-archival workshop papers (per NeurIPS 2026 workshop policy).

We welcome research, position, dataset/benchmark, and systems contributions on AI for aging and intervention modeling. We will not accept work already published at major ML venues or presented at the NeurIPS 2026 main conference.

Format (planned): posters for accepted papers; a subset invited for spotlight talks.
