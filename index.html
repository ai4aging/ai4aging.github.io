Proposed NeurIPS 2026 Workshop

Foundation Models and Generative AI for Longevity Science

ML for aging biology: virtual cells, perturbations, and intervention modeling.

An informal venue for discussion, community building, and work in progress at the intersection of machine learning and longevity science.

Status: Workshop proposal in preparation. NeurIPS decisions expected July 11, 2026.

About

Why now?

Foundation models, generative AI, and virtual-cell systems are rapidly changing how we model cells and interventions—but longevity science needs its own ML community: aging is multi-scale and heterogeneous, and intervention discovery demands uncertainty-aware, biologically grounded methods.

This workshop will bring together ML researchers and biologists to discuss modeling aging and rejuvenation, predicting perturbation responses, and evaluating AI systems for longevity-relevant discovery. We aim for substantial discussion time, contributed posters and spotlights, and panels—not a talks-only program.

Related NeurIPS workshops (e.g. virtual cells for drug discovery) focus on D&D pipelines; we center aging biology, clocks, and intervention modeling as a distinct ML problem.

Topics

Areas of interest

  • Biological foundation models and multimodal cell-state representations
  • Virtual cells, perturbation prediction, and generative intervention design
  • Causal ML, experimental design, and aging clocks / biomarkers
  • Benchmarks, reproducibility, and biologically meaningful evaluation

Call for Papers

Contributions (after acceptance)

Submission details will be posted here if the workshop is accepted. We plan to use OpenReview, with non-archival workshop papers (per NeurIPS 2026 workshop policy).

We welcome research, position, dataset/benchmark, and systems contributions on AI for aging and intervention modeling. We will not accept work already published at major ML venues or presented at the NeurIPS 2026 main conference.

Format (planned): posters for accepted papers; a subset invited for spotlight talks.
